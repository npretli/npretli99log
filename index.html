Workflow Documentation for creating an ArcGIS Dashboard

Workflow

This serves as documentation for how I went about creating ArcGIS Dashboards. Ideas were sourced from learning resources provided by this course and instructional demos provided in GEOM 108. All screen captures are my own.

Step 1: Open your ArcGIS Pro Project / Workspace.

Step 2: Note the name of your geodatabase in the Catalog Pane.

Step 3: From File Explorer, zip the contents of your geodatabase folder.

Step 4: Sign in to ArcGIS Online.

Step 5: Under the content pane, select "Add Item", followed by "From your computer"

Step 6: Under File, navigate to the location where your geodatabase is stored on your personal PC. Under Contents, select File Geodatabase, and provide your geodatabase a title. Specify a tag.

Step 7: Select on the map thumbnail to open the web map.

Step 8: In this environment, the symbology and feature class titles will first need to be modified prior to the creation of the dashboard itself.

First, begin by clicking on a feature class of choice in the Contents pane - in this scenario, we'll begin with symbology (3rd symbol from the left).

Step 9: Select an attribute of interest, followed by a drawing style of interest.

Step 10: Select "Classify Data"

Step 11: Select the number of classes to be rendered.

Then, click on "Legend"

Step 12: Specify and edit the value ranges along the colour ramp as necessary (the colour ramp itself can also be modified from under "Symbols").

Step 13: Within the web map: select Basemap, and under the drop-down menu, select "Dark Gray Canvas" (or other basemap of choice)

Step 14: Note the name of your geodatabase in the Catalog Pane.

Step 15: Be sure to save your map layers individually as you edit each one (I've forgotten to do this myself - more than once!) Once this is complete, be sure to save the web map itself.

Step 16: Click on the applications drop-down menu (symbol with nine dots), and select "Dashboards"

Step 17: Provide a name for your dashboard, a tag, and a summary before proceeding.

Step 18: On the '+' drop-down menu, select "Map", and select the map of interest from your ArcGIS Portal (i.e., the web map from earlier).

Step 19: Under the Settings pane, toggle all of the features on. Leave the point zoom scale as the default.

Step 20: On the '+' drop-down menu, select "Map legend"

Step 21: Set the text colour to white and the background colour to black. Add a title and provide a description if desired.

Step 22: Add a description/supplementary information to the legend if necessary

Step 23: On the '+' drop-down menu, select "Indicator"

Step 24: Choose a feature to select for the Indicator

Step 25: Under the Data tab, fill out the appropriate fields (modify the 'Statistic' and 'Field' options as desired)

Step 26: Under the Indicator tab, insert text (top & bottom text) as desired.

Step 27: Under the General pane, change the text colour to white and the background colour to black.

Step 28: Under the '+' drop-down menu, select "List"

Step 29: Choose a feature to select for display on the List

Step 30: Under the List tab, select field attributes for display for this feature class (e.g., name and address)

Step 31: Under the General tab, provide a title for the list. Also modify the text colour to white and the background colour to black

Step 32: Under the General tab, modify the text colour to white and the background colour to black.

Step 33: Create additional lists for other map features as desired

Step 34: Under the blue ribbon on the top-left of the map, select the wrench ("Configure")

Step 35: Under the Map actions pane, select "Add target"

Step 36: Continue to populate features to target.

(This functionality enables the indicator to reflect only those features visible on the map - meaning that statistics adjust based on your zoom level.)

Once all the desired targets have been populated, click Done.

Step 37: Under the '+' drop-down menu, select "List"

Step 38: Add a title & subtitle. Modify the text colour to white and background colour to black if necessary.

Step 39: Drag the columns to adjust their sizes as they are populated on the dashboard. Be sure to save your work.

Step 39: Notice the indicator modifies with the zoom level (in this example, it reflects the count of parks currently visible in the map frame at this particular zoom level).
